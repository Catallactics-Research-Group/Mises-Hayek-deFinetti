## Proposed Structure for the JEP Proposal (~3 pages)

---

**1. Opening Hook (1-2 paragraphs)**

- JEP explicitly seeks "intellectual arbitrage" — this paper delivers a *literal* arbitrage across three fields.
- Lead with the Buchanan quote: "Economics is about arbitrage." Establish the puzzle: three major research programs (Austrian catallactics, de Finetti's subjective probability, modern finance) independently arrived at no-arbitrage as their organizing principle without recognizing each other.
- State the thesis bluntly: we unify them, and the unification is not merely taxonomic — it produces new results.

---

**2. The Arbitrage Trinity (3-4 paragraphs, one per pillar)**

- **Austrian catallactics:** Arbitrage is the driving force of the market process (Mises, Kirzner). Contrast with Walrasian no-arbitrage-as-equilibrium. The catallaxy *runs on* arbitrage; Walrasian economics *assumes it away*.
- **De Finetti:** The Dutch book argument defines coherent probability as a no-arbitrage condition on beliefs. Probability, price, and prevision are interchangeable. This is Austrian economics with different notation — a social, interactive, non-frequentist foundation for uncertainty.
- **Finance:** The Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing as no-arbitrage pricing of contingent claims. The mathematical apex of the trinity.

Keep this brief. The editors don't need the arguments made — they need to see that you can make them accessibly.

---

**3. The Central Synthesis (2-3 paragraphs)**

- The FTP=FTAP equivalence: de Finetti's Fundamental Theorem of Prevision is identical in structure to the Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing. State this clearly and let it land. This is the paper's most striking claim.
- But the two theorems operate at different levels: individual (de Finetti) vs. market (FTAP). The gap between them *just is* the Hayekian knowledge-and-coordination problem. Bridging them means explaining how subjective individual beliefs aggregate into market prices — which is precisely what the catallaxy does.
- This provides deeper arbitrage foundations for finance than Arrow-Debreu or rational expectations can supply, and it grounds the critique of those frameworks.

---

**4. The Inversion: Options as Search, Not Search as Options (2 paragraphs)**

- The standard move exploits the options/search isomorphism by importing finance math into search theory. We go the other direction.
- Options market-making, understood through de Finetti + Austrian entrepreneurship, is a highly specialized case of search in a highly scaffolded institutional setting. The empirical success of quantitative finance is not a vindication of its theoretical foundations — it is an advertisement for catallactic institutions. The math works because the exchange, standardized contracts, and continuous price discovery have done enormous coordinating work. Strip the scaffolding and you have job search, which the same math cannot touch. This is a key catallactic insight.

---

**5. Computational Statistical Catallactics as Methodology (1-2 paragraphs)**

- ACE is a method without a theory. Catallactics is a theory without a quantitatively operational method. We combine them.
- This methodology enables a concrete empirical demonstration: agent-based models with subjective decision-making under uncertainty and variable levels of institutional scaffolding. As scaffolding increases, options-style math becomes progressively more predictive. This operationalizes the catallactic insight and gives the paper an empirical anchor.

---

**6. Payoff and Contribution (1 paragraph)**

- Briefly state what each of the three literatures gains from the synthesis.
- Austrian market process theory gets an operational, computational methodology and a rebuilt capital theory via real options on subjective foundations.
- De Finetti's program gets a macroeconomic interpretation it has lacked.
- Finance gets a deeper institutional and epistemological foundation that explains *why* it works where it works.

---

**7. Brief Note on Accessibility and Style (2-3 sentences)**

- Acknowledge the technical range of the material and reassure the editors that the argument does not require readers to know measure theory or Austrian capital theory — the through-line is the arbitrage concept, which any economist will recognize.
