## What a JEP Proposal Is (and Isn't)

The critical insight is that **a JEP proposal is not a mini paper**. It's closer to a well-argued pitch that serves two functions simultaneously: it tells the editors what the article will *argue* and what the article will *feel like* to read. JEP editors are asking: "Can this author pull off an accessible, opinionated synthesis that crosses subspecialty lines?" They're evaluating both the idea and your ability to execute it in JEP style. 

The distinction matters for your outline. A mini paper would front-load the theoretical apparatus (Dutch books, Radon-Nikodym derivatives, no-arbitrage conditions). A good JEP proposal inverts that — it leads with the *intellectual stakes* and the *unifying insight*, and only gestures at the technical machinery to show you know what you're doing.

## Proposed Strategy for the Proposal

I'd structure it as roughly **3 tight pages** with this architecture:

**1. The Hook (half a page)**
Open with the observation that JEP explicitly seeks "intellectual arbitrage" — and then observe that your paper delivers a literal arbitrage: a unified foundation connecting three fields (Austrian catallactics, de Finetti's subjective probability, modern finance) that have developed largely in isolation. The Buchanan quote ("Economics is about arbitrage") is your lede. It's punchy, surprising, and sets up everything.

**2. The Central Claim (~half a page)**
State the thesis bluntly and opinionatedly, as JEP requires. Something like: the no-arbitrage condition is not merely a technical convenience in finance — it is the foundational organizing principle of a coherent research program that unifies market process theory, subjective probability, and asset pricing. De Finetti turns out to have been doing Austrian economics with different notation, and neither camp noticed.

**3. The Three Pillars, Briefly (~1 page)**
One short paragraph per pillar: catallactics (Mises/Kirzner arbitrage theory of profit), de Finetti (Dutch book as no-arbitrage condition on coherent beliefs, probability = price = prevision), and finance (fundamental theorem of asset pricing). The key move is showing the *parallelism* explicitly — each field independently arrived at no-arbitrage as its organizing principle. That's the surprise that justifies a JEP article.

**4. The Synthesis and Stakes (~half a page)**
What does unifying these three accomplish that none of the individual literatures could do alone? This is where the real options section and the computational catallactics ideas could appear — not as developed arguments, but as gestures toward implications. JEP editors want to know the *payoff*.

**5. A Word on Accessibility and Audience (~quarter page)**
Explicitly address the JEP challenge: this material is technical, but the *argument* doesn't require the reader to know measure theory. Reassure them that the Radon-Nikodym derivative, for instance, will be explained through economic intuition about pricing under uncertainty rather than through a proof.
